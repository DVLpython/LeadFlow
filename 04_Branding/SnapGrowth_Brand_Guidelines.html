Color System

A warm, pastel-forward palette built on coral energy and amber warmth. Blush and sage provide breathing room. Every color is chosen to feel approachable and human — not cold, corporate, or generic.

Coral is for action
Use coral for CTAs, links, active states, and key emphasis. It signals energy, urgency, and warmth. Never use it for large backgrounds on important text — contrast is critical.
Blush is for breathing
Use blush and cream for backgrounds, cards, and subtle fills. They give the eye a place to rest. Blush is the pastel foundation that makes the coral pop without overwhelming.
Sage is for positivity
Use sage sparingly for success states, positive indicators, and cool-temperature contrast. It balances the warm palette and prevents it from feeling one-dimensional.

Logo Usage

Consistent, respectful use of the logo protects the brand's visual equity. These rules exist so the mark always looks intentional.

CLEAR SPACE
× height of mark
× height of mark
SnapGrowth

Always maintain clear space equal to the height of the symbol mark on all four sides. No other elements — text, images, or decorative elements — should enter this zone.

✓ Do
Use the logo on white, cream, blush, dark, and coral backgrounds
Use the gradient mark on light backgrounds and white/ghost mark on dark or coral
Maintain the wordmark in Fraunces Bold only — never substitute another typeface
Scale proportionally — lock the aspect ratio always
Use the symbol mark alone only at sizes where the wordmark becomes illegible (<120px wide)
✕ Don't
Stretch, squish, or rotate the logo
Change any of the logo colors individually
Add drop shadows, outlines, or effects to the logo
Place the logo on a busy photo or patterned background without a clear zone
Use the logo at sizes smaller than 120px wide for the full lockup
Recreate the logo in another application — always use the approved SVG file
05

Brand Voice

SnapGrowth sounds like a confident friend who happens to be an expert. Direct without being blunt. Warm without being fluffy. The guarantee means every word has to earn its place.

🎯
Direct
Not → blunt or aggressive
We say what we mean. No jargon, no padding, no hedging. If we guarantee 12 meetings, we say "12 meetings" — not "approximately 10-14 qualified interactions per calendar period."
☀️
Warm
Not → cheesy or sycophantic
We genuinely care about client success. The warmth comes from confidence, not flattery. We don't say "great question!" We say "here's the answer."
⚡
Energetic
Not → breathless or hyped
There's momentum in everything we write. Short sentences. Active voice. Verbs that move. We don't describe what we do — we show what happens next for the client.
TONE IN ACTION
✓ SnapGrowth says this
"We guarantee 12 qualified meetings per month — or the next month is free. First campaigns live in 7 days."
✕ Not this
"Our AI-powered platform leverages cutting-edge machine learning algorithms to potentially optimise your lead generation funnel."
✓ SnapGrowth says this
"Your team should be closing deals, not making cold calls. We fill the calendar — you focus on the handshake."
✕ Not this
"We are pleased to offer a comprehensive suite of B2B demand generation solutions designed to enhance your sales pipeline efficiency."
